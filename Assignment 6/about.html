Alpha Phi Omega is a co-ed volunteering and service fraternity on many campuses around the country. It was founded in 1925 by Frank Reed Horton at Lafayette College. APO was founded with the goal of developing Leadership, promoting Friendship, and providing Service to humanity. It is the largest collegiate fraternity in the United States, with chapters at over 350 campuses, an active membership of over 25,000 students, and over 400,000 alumni members. There are also 250 chapters in the Philippines, one in Australia and one in Canada.


The Xi Upsilon chapter at Mount Union was founded in 1998 by Mr. and Mrs. Miner after their son passed away in a car accident. We celebrated our 20th anniversary last semester. We currently have 40 active brothers and we are expecting more with the incoming pledge class.


We do many service projects for the chapter, university, community, and country. We also have lots of social events for members to attend.
